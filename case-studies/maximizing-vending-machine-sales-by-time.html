Maximizing Vending Machine Sales by Time

Boost sales and restocking efficiency by understanding when customers buy most and aligning promotions with time-of-day patterns.

1. Identifying Sales Patterns

This case study examines vending machine sales across morning, afternoon, and evening time blocks to help businesses make smarter restocking, pricing, and promotional decisions.

2. Key Results

  • Revenue by Time of Day:
    • Afternoon: $50,108.80
    • Morning: $39,106.20
    • Evening: $33,106.58

    Afternoons are best for volume deals, while mornings work better with premium pricing.

  • Most Popular Drinks by Time:
    • Morning: Americano (350), Americano variant (232), Café Latte (145)
    • Afternoon: Latte (335), Americano (313), Café Latte (213)
    • Evening: Latte (233), Americano (205), Café Latte (172)

    Americano, Latte, and Café Latte dominate across all time blocks.

  • Low-Performing Drinks: Irish whiskey, chocolate, mocha chip, caramel with chocolate, and double latte sell poorly, especially in the evening.

3. Aligning Insights with Business Goals

  • Optimize Sales: Time promotions around peak revenue hours to boost results.
  • Improve Efficiency: Restock smarter by focusing on top drinks at specific times.
  • Maximize Promotions: Target the right products at the right time to improve engagement.

Impact:

  • Revenue Growth: Afternoons are the highest-revenue slot—perfect for bundling offers.
  • Efficient Restocking: Focus stock on Americano and Latte, especially during peak hours.
  • Better Campaigns: Use time-of-day data to match offers with customer moods and preferences.

4. Interpreting Time-of-Day Purchase Behavior

  • Time Variations: Behavior shifts by time—afternoons favor bulk buying, mornings suit premium drinks.
  • Customer Favorites: Americano, Latte, and Café Latte remain top choices and should be consistently stocked and promoted.
  • Struggling Drinks: Low sales for niche drinks may require rebranding or strategic promotion.

Context:

  • Behavior: Strong coffee in the morning, smoother drinks later—promotions should reflect this.
  • Promotion Timing: Smart scheduling can lift sales and improve brand perception.

5. Strategic Recommendations

  • Time-Based Promotions:
    • Morning – promote premium drinks like Americano: “Morning Espresso Kick.”
    • Afternoon – volume deals like “Latte Lounge Hour.”
    • Evening – try “Smooth Latte Wind-Down” for lighter drinks.
  • Smart Restocking: Prioritize top-sellers like Americano and Latte during high-demand hours.
  • Low Seller Strategy: Test limited offers, reformulations, or removal for persistently underperforming drinks.
  • Test New Items at Off-Peak: Use quiet evening periods to introduce new drinks or drive promotions.

6. Conclusion

This case study highlights the power of time-based data to improve vending operations. By aligning product availability and promotions with actual purchase behavior, vending businesses can grow revenue, reduce waste, and better satisfy customers.

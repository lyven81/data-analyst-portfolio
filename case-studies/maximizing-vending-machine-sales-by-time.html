Maximizing Vending Machine Sales by Time

Understand customer behavior across morning, afternoon, and evening to drive smarter sales, marketing, and restocking strategies.

Overview

The goal of this analysis is to understand customer behavior and sales trends based on different time blocks throughout the day—morning, afternoon, and evening. The aim is to provide actionable insights that help optimize product offerings, promotions, and restocking schedules for a coffee vending machine business.

Key Results

Revenue by Time of Day:

  • Afternoon: $50,108.80 (highest revenue)
  • Morning: $39,106.20
  • Evening: $33,106.58

Afternoon is ideal for volume promotions, while morning suits premium offers due to higher AOV.

Most Popular Drinks by Time of Day:

Morning: Americano (350), Americano variant (232), Café Latte (145)

Afternoon: Latte (335), Americano (313), Café Latte (213)

Evening: Latte (233), Americano (205), Café Latte (172)

Low-Performing Drinks:

Drinks like Irish whiskey, chocolate, mocha chip, caramel with chocolate, and double latte had very low frequency, especially in the evening.

Goals Alignment

  • Optimizing Sales: Use peak sales data for timing promotions and restocking.
  • Improving Operational Efficiency: Stock high-demand drinks and minimize waste.
  • Maximizing Promotions: Promote the right drinks at the right times.

Impact

  • Revenue Growth: Leverage high-revenue afternoon periods with targeted offers.
  • Efficiency in Restocking: Align stock levels with time-based demand.
  • Marketing Effectiveness: Tailor promotions by time block to boost engagement.

Data Interpretation

Time-of-Day Variations: Afternoon drives more volume, morning has higher AOV—indicating need for different strategies.

Product Preferences: Americano, Latte, and Café Latte are top sellers across all time blocks.

Low-Performing Drinks: Irish whiskey and double latte may require rebranding, discounts, or removal.

Contextual Factors

  • Customer Behavior: Strong coffee in the morning, lighter choices in the afternoon/evening.
  • Promotional Impact: Preferences vary with time—ideal for segmented marketing.

Insight Generation

This analysis uncovers strong patterns in purchase timing and drink popularity, giving clear opportunities to drive sales, minimize waste, and boost satisfaction through tailored product strategies.

Recommendations

  • Targeted Promotions:
    • Morning: Promote premium drinks (e.g., “Morning Espresso Kick”).
    • Afternoon: Run volume-based promotions (e.g., “Latte Lounge Hour”).
    • Evening: Offer relaxed, lighter drink specials (e.g., “Smooth Latte Wind-Down”).
  • Restocking Strategy: Focus on Americano and Latte during peak hours.
  • Address Low-Performing Products: Test limited-time offers or remove low performers.
  • Experiment with Low-Sales Hours: Use evening periods to test new drinks and promotions.

Conclusion

The findings from this analysis offer actionable insights for optimizing sales, restocking schedules, and promotional strategies based on time-of-day purchase behavior. By aligning marketing efforts with customer preferences and focusing on both high-performing and underperforming products, the business can drive revenue growth while maintaining operational efficiency. Moving forward, these insights will enable more data-driven decisions that align with customer behavior and maximize overall sales potential.

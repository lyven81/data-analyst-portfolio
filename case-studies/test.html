Maximizing Revenue with Data-Driven Price Adjustment for Skincare Products

In this study, we use data-driven insights to understand how adjusting prices impacts sales for skincare products. We examine how raising prices by 10% could impact sales volume and help skincare retailers optimize their pricing strategies for better profitability.

Executive Summary

This case study focuses on predicting sales volume for skincare products and evaluating how price changes affect sales. By using a linear regression model, we predicted sales volume with high accuracy. We found that some products can maintain sales despite price increases, offering clear insights for improving pricing strategies.

Problem Statement

The skincare retailer needs to find out which products can handle a 10% price increase without reducing demand. This will help them raise profit margins without losing customers.

Approach

We used a linear regression model to predict sales for skincare products. The model had an RMSE of 270.71, showing it can accurately predict sales volume. We focused on the top 5 best-selling products and analyzed how a 10% price increase would affect their sales compared to competitors.

Results

Top 5 Best-Selling Products

  • Neutrogena Hydro Boost Gel: 98,635 units
  • Eucerin Advanced Repair Lotion: 95,542 units
  • Drunk Elephant Vitamin C Serum: 91,308 units
  • Cetaphil Gentle Cleanser: 87,281 units
  • Bioderma Sensibio H2O: 80,399 units

Impact of Price Increases on Sales

Neutrogena and Eucerin products are expected to increase sales despite a 10% price hike. However, Cetaphil, Drunk Elephant, and Bioderma are likely to see a drop in sales volume after the price increase.

Key Insights

  • Neutrogena Hydro Boost Gel and Eucerin Advanced Repair Lotion can handle price increases while maintaining sales, making them great options for margin improvements.
  • Cetaphil Gentle Cleanser and Bioderma Sensibio H2O are more price-sensitive, so price increases should be approached cautiously.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Increase Prices for Key Products: Focus on raising prices for Neutrogena and Eucerin, as they can keep sales steady with higher prices.
  • Be Cautious with Sensitive Products: Products like Cetaphil and Bioderma may lose sales if prices rise too much. Be strategic with price increases for these items.
  • Stay Aware of Competitors: As price increases may lead to higher prices than competitors, the retailer should ensure they remain competitive.

Conclusion

The linear regression model shows that Neutrogena and Eucerin can handle price increases while still maintaining high sales, making them ideal for higher margins. However, the retailer must be cautious with price-sensitive products like Cetaphil and Bioderma to avoid losing customers.

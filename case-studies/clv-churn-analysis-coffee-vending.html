Saving the Silent Spenders in Coffee Vending Machines

Identifying Churn Risks and Revenue Opportunities Through CLV Insights

1. Executive Summary

This report presents the findings of a customer lifetime value (CLV) and segmentation analysis conducted for a coffee vending machine business. The objective is to identify high-value customers, detect churn risks, and uncover revenue growth opportunities through targeted retention and upsell strategies.

2. Key Findings

2.1 Top 10 Customers by Predicted Lifetime Value

Top predicted CLV ranges from $370.94 to $3,662.98, identifying high-potential customers for loyalty rewards and engagement.

2.2 Customer Segmentation by Spending Tier

  • Tier 4 (Highest): Avg. Spend $2,259.01
  • Tier 3: Avg. Spend $497.53
  • Tier 2: Avg. Spend $60.29
  • Tier 1: Avg. Spend $50.70

2.3 High Frequency, Low Spend Customers

Frequent but low-value customers present upsell potential through bundles or incentives.

2.4 Recent Buyers with Low Frequency

New or occasional buyers are good candidates for onboarding and time-sensitive engagement campaigns.

2.5 Churn Risks Among High-Value Customers

Some high-CLV customers show no recent activity—indicating a need for reactivation strategies.

2.6 Behavioral Patterns Linked to Higher CLV

Tier 4 customers share traits of low recency, high frequency, and high spend—useful for modeling and targeting.

3. Strategic Relevance to Business Goals

  • Customer Retention: Intervene early with churn-prone customers.
  • Revenue Growth: Use segmentation to drive conversions and LTV.
  • Operational Efficiency: Align stock to customer behavior.
  • Marketing Optimization: Personalize campaigns with behavior data.

4. Business Implications and Recommendations

  • VIP Loyalty Program: Reward Tier 4 customers with exclusive perks.
  • Upsell & Bundle Offers: Target frequent low-spenders with spend-based incentives.
  • Churn Prevention: Send re-engagement offers to inactive high-value customers.
  • Inventory Optimization: Stock high-demand drinks aligned to top-segment preferences.
  • Behavior-Informed Campaigns: Encourage Tier 4 behavior in lower tiers.

5. Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates that a focused approach to CLV, customer segmentation, and behavior modeling can lead to significant improvements in revenue retention, customer loyalty, and operational efficiency. By implementing the recommended actions, the business can better engage its most valuable customers, recover at-risk segments, and lay a data-driven foundation for sustainable growth.

Saving the Silent Spenders in Coffee Vending Machines

Uncover hidden revenue by identifying at-risk high-value customers and keeping them engaged through targeted strategies.

1. Uncovering Revenue Potential and Churn Risk

This analysis explores customer purchasing behavior in a coffee vending machine business, using CLV estimation and segmentation to identify churn risks and optimize marketing efforts.

2. Key Results

  • Top 10 Customers by Predicted CLV: CLVs range from $370.94 to $3,662.98 — valuable targets for loyalty campaigns.
  • Customer Segmentation:
    • Tier 4: High-value, avg. spend $2,259.01
    • Tier 3: Moderate spenders, good upsell candidates
    • Tier 1 & 2: Low spenders, potential for growth via promotions
  • High Frequency, Low Spenders: Some frequent customers spend little per purchase — ideal for bundle offers or incentives.
  • Recent but Low Frequency: New or infrequent buyers could be nurtured with welcome offers and onboarding campaigns.
  • Churn Risk: High CLV customers with no recent activity need targeted reactivation strategies.
  • CLV Behavioral Patterns: Tier 4 customers exhibit low recency, high frequency, and high spend — prime loyalty program candidates.

3. Translating Insights into Business Value

  • Customer Retention: Targeting churn-prone but valuable customers helps maintain long-term revenue.
  • Revenue Growth: CLV-based segmentation guides more effective promotions.
  • Operational Efficiency: Stock machines with popular products and reduce waste from underperformers.
  • Marketing Optimization: Use behavior-based segmentation for campaign personalization.

Impact:

  • Targeted Marketing: Campaigns aligned with customer value tiers yield better results.
  • Churn Prevention: Reactivating top spenders prevents lost revenue.
  • Smarter Inventory: Prioritize items popular with high-value and frequent buyers.

4. Understanding the Drivers Behind

  • High CLV Customers: Repeat purchases + high spend = long-term value.
  • Segment Insights: Tier 4 leads in recency, frequency, and spend — others need nurturing.
  • Spend vs. Frequency: A positive correlation, but some need help spending more per visit.
  • Churn Signals: High CLV but no recent activity signals urgent need for reactivation campaigns.

Contextual Factors:

  • Seasonality: Customer value may shift during holidays or sales.
  • Past Promotions: Incentives may influence frequency and recency patterns.

5. Strategic Recommendations

  • Launch VIP Loyalty Program: Reward Tier 4 customers with exclusive perks.
  • Upsell High-Frequency, Low-Spend Customers: Offer bundles or bonus points to boost per-purchase spend.
  • Reactivate Churn Risks: Send personalized offers to high CLV customers who’ve gone silent.
  • Optimize Inventory: Keep high-demand drinks available, cut low performers.
  • Use Behavioral Modeling: Target campaigns that replicate top-tier behaviors across all segments.

6. Conclusion

This case study demonstrates how data on CLV and customer behavior can help boost revenue, reduce churn, and create smarter campaigns. Focusing on value-based segmentation enables the business to grow more strategically by nurturing its most valuable customers and converting occasional users into loyal buyers.

Peaking in 2008, Flattening After 2017: What Happened to Global Video Game Sales?

Understand the rise, fall, and stagnation of global video game sales and use these insights to time launches, adapt strategies, and uncover new growth areas.

1. Identifying Global Sales

This analysis explores global video game sales trends over time, identifying key moments of growth and stagnation to support smarter decision-making for launch timing, investment, and strategy in the video game industry.

2. Key Results

  • Peak and Low Sales Years:
    • 2008 marked the highest global video game sales—an era of rapid growth.
    • 2020 saw the lowest reported sales likely due to data gaps during the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • Year-over-Year Sales Evolution:
    • 1980–2008 saw rapid fluctuations and growth.
    • Post-2008, sales stabilized and began to stagnate—especially after 2017.
    • 2020 spiked due to pandemic-driven gaming behavior.
  • Sales Spikes and Drops:
    • Spikes: 2007–2008, 2020 – linked to platform launches and global events.
    • Drops: 2012–2017 – tied to mobile gaming growth and console transition periods.
  • Long-Term Trends: Sales growth has plateaued since 2017, suggesting industry maturity and shifting consumer preferences.

3. Connecting Sales Trends to Strategic Business Goals

Goals Alignment:

  • Improve Forecasting: Identify peak/slow years to plan marketing and inventory.
  • Guide Product Launch Timing: Launch during high-opportunity periods, avoid slumps.
  • Optimize Investment: Invest in the right platforms and growth areas using sales trends.

Impact:

  • Capitalize on pandemic-era momentum with digital-first offerings and contingency planning.
  • Address post-2017 stagnation by reassessing platforms, pricing, and customer targeting.

4. Interpreting Market Shifts Behind the Trends

  • 2008 Spike: Driven by console popularity and broader market adoption.
  • 2020 Spike: COVID-19 lockdowns increased demand for indoor entertainment.
  • Post-2008 Decline & 2017 Plateau:
    • Rise of mobile gaming and freemium models.
    • Console lifecycle gaps.
    • Industry maturity and market saturation.

Contextual Factors:

  • Shifts to mobile, cross-platform, and free-to-play models reshaped demand.
  • Economic uncertainty (e.g. pandemic) altered gaming habits and platform preferences.

5. Strategic Recommendations

  • Leverage Pandemic Trends: Prioritize digital-first, flexible gaming models (e.g., streaming, subscriptions).
  • Focus on Emerging Markets: Tap into high-growth regions like Asia and Latin America.
  • Invest in Mobile: Expand into mobile-first experiences and cross-platform play.
  • Time Product Launches Strategically: Use historical sales data to hit high-performing windows.
  • Adopt Alternative Revenue Streams: Shift from traditional sales to in-game purchases, esports, and memberships.

6. Conclusion

This case study reveals how global gaming sales have evolved, highlighting the importance of flexible, data-driven strategies in today’s maturing industry. To thrive, businesses must act on emerging patterns, invest in mobile and emerging markets, and rethink launch timing and monetization models to align with shifting consumer habits.

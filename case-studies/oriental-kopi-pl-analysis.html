The Business Question

Oriental Kopi Holdings Bhd is a Malaysian kopitiam-style café chain and packaged-food distributor listed on Bursa Malaysia. In 1Q FY2026 the group reported a 42% jump in revenue year-on-year — yet management's real question was sharper: if we grew this fast, why did profit margin fall?

This analysis reads the quarterly profit & loss the way a management team would — vertical analysis (every line as a share of revenue), horizontal analysis (year-on-year change), and a segment breakdown — to locate exactly where the margin went.

What the Numbers Say

  • Growth is real, but profit grew slower than revenue. Revenue rose 42.3% while profit after tax rose 30.2% — the gap is the margin squeeze.
  • The squeeze is in cost of sales. Cost of sales climbed from 74.1% to 77.0% of revenue, pulling gross margin down from 25.9% to 23.0%. Every margin tier — gross, operating, net — compressed.
  • Distribution & Retail is the growth engine. That segment more than doubled (+125.6%), while the Cafe Chain — still ~90% of revenue — grew 37%.
  • But every segment's margin fell. Cafe Chain 24.3% → 21.2%, Distribution & Retail 53.3% → 42.3%, Others 16.1% → 15.0%. The mix shift toward distribution is part of the story, but margin pressure is broad-based.

Method

Three clean tables were extracted from the published quarterly report: the full P&L statement (12 line items, two periods), a 3-quarter headline trend, and a segment breakdown. The dashboard computes vertical analysis (% of revenue), horizontal analysis (YoY %), margin ratios, and segment growth/margin change entirely in the browser. The same outline was also built as a Power BI model (DAX measures for vertical/horizontal/margin analysis).

Note: the headline trend covers three non-consecutive quarters (Dec'24 → Sep'25 → Dec'25); the emphasis is year-on-year and structural comparison rather than a continuous time series.

What Management Should Do

  • Protect gross margin. The 2.9-ppt rise in cost of sales is the single biggest profit leak — investigate input costs, pricing, and product/segment mix before chasing more top-line growth.
  • Watch the mix shift. Distribution & Retail is growing fastest but at a falling margin; confirm it is still accretive to group profitability as it scales.
  • Track margin quality alongside growth. Revenue growth alone masked a profitability slip this quarter — pair every growth target with a margin guardrail.

The Vanishing Profit

How a 12% sales rise earned almost nothing

Data Stories LabAnalyst report2Q FY2026 · RM’000
Analyst ReportDashboard
⌂ Home

MyNews sold more in the quarter to April 2026 than a year earlier, with revenue up almost 12% as it added stores. Yet by the time the numbers reached the bottom line, almost nothing was left: profit after tax fell from RM2.1M to RM67,000. This report lifts the lid on a thin-margin retailer to show how a small slip in the margin, and the cost of growing, can swallow a whole quarter's profit, and leaves the why to those who run the business.

What the data shows

What we found

MyNews Holdings runs more than 730 convenience outlets across Malaysia under brands including myNEWS, CU, WHSmith and SUPERVALUE, plus its own ready-to-eat food and bakery hub. In the quarter to 30 April 2026 it grew revenue 11.6%, from RM202.6M to RM226.0M, by opening stores. But profit after tax fell 96.8%, to just RM67,000.

That gap between a healthy sales rise and a near-zero profit is the whole story. It opens in two places: the margin the company earns on each sale, which slipped, and the cost of running and growing the network, which rose. On a business that keeps only a sliver of each sales ringgit, both together were enough to take the quarter's profit.

1. The bottom line nearly vanished

Sales rose, profit collapsed
Year-on-year change, by line
Source: MyNews Holdings, 2Q FY2026 interim report.

Revenue grew 11.6% and gross profit a slower 6.0%, but profit before tax fell 70.6% and profit after tax 96.8%. Each step down the statement, the picture worsened. When the bottom line falls this far while the top line rises, the cause is not demand; it is what happens to each ringgit between the sale and the profit.

2. A thinner margin on each sale

The margin slipped at the top, and ran out at the bottom
Margin at each level, this quarter vs a year ago
Source: MyNews Holdings, 2Q FY2026 interim report.

Gross margin contracted from 39.5% to 37.5%, a two-point slip the company attributes to a change in product mix and to in-store promotions. That alone matters more than it sounds: by the time the usual running costs are taken out, the pre-tax margin fell from 2.3% of sales to 0.6%, and the net margin from 1.0% to almost zero. There is very little cushion on a margin this thin.

3. Where the margin went

Cost of sales climbed, and so did the cost of growing
Each cost as a share of revenue, this quarter vs a year ago
Source: MyNews Holdings, 2Q FY2026 interim report.

Reading each line as a share of revenue locates both pressures. Cost of sales rose to 62.5% of revenue from 60.5%, which is the two-point gross-margin slip. Above that, the cost of growing showed up: administrative expenses grew 20% (the company cites hiring in automation, artificial intelligence and its commercial team), and selling and distribution expenses grew 12.7% as it opened 65 new outlets. Sales grew 11.6%; these costs grew faster.

4. From a ringgit of sales to almost nothing

Revenue to net profit bridge
How RM226.0M of revenue becomes RM0.07M of net profit (RM’000)
Source: MyNews Holdings, 2Q FY2026 interim report.

The bridge makes the thinness visible. Cost of sales takes the largest bite, then selling and distribution, then the other running costs, and the bar is on the floor before tax is even taken. For this business the last sliver is so small that any movement in the bars above it shows up, magnified, at the bottom.

5. Profit gone, but the cash kept coming

Accounting profit near zero, cash earnings still large
Quarterly EBITDA versus profit after tax (RM’000)
Source: MyNews Holdings, 2Q FY2026 interim report.

One number keeps the quarter in perspective. While profit after tax was RM67,000, the business still earned about RM28M before interest, tax and depreciation, and generated RM62M of operating cash over the six months. The difference is depreciation: a store network built on leased space and fitted-out outlets carries very heavy non-cash charges, which sit between the cash the tills take and the profit the accounts report. The profit nearly vanished; the cash did not.

6. One business, and a seasonally weak quarter

A seasonal low point
Revenue and profit after tax, last three reported quarters (RM’000)
Source: MyNews Holdings, 2Q FY2026 interim report.

Almost all of the group's outside sales come from its retail stores; a small manufacturing arm (its Japanese Food Hub) mostly supplies those stores rather than selling outside, so the group's result is, in practice, the retail result. The quarter was also the seasonal low point: it spanned the Ramadan month and a short February, which the company flags as softening sales. Over the full six months, profit fell a milder 28%, so part of this quarter's collapse is the calendar, not only the structure.

What the numbers reveal

Put together, the quarter tells one clear factual story. Sales grew, but the profit nearly disappeared because two things moved against a wafer-thin margin at once: the gross margin slipped two points, and the cost of running and expanding the network grew faster than sales. On a business that turns barely a cent of each sales ringgit into pre-tax profit, that is all it takes. Underneath the accounting result, the business still threw off real cash, and the quarter was a seasonal low.

The shape worth keeping in view is the thinness itself. A retailer with a sub-1% net margin has almost no buffer, so every small move in price, mix or cost lands, magnified, at the bottom line. Whether the margin slip and the rising costs are the temporary price of opening stores and running promotions, or a more lasting squeeze, is the question the numbers here cannot settle.

Questions the numbers raise
  • Is the two-point gross-margin slip a deliberate, temporary trade (promotions and mix to drive footfall in new stores) that reverses, or a lasting change in what the business can charge?
  • The new outlets and the hiring in automation and AI cost money now; will they earn enough later to lift the margin back, or keep weighing on it?
  • How much of this quarter's near-zero profit is the Ramadan and short-February calendar, given the six-month figure fell far less?
Method & data

Built from MyNews Holdings' unaudited interim report for the second quarter ended 30 April 2026, read against the same quarter a year earlier. From the profit and loss statement it computes vertical analysis (each line as a share of revenue), horizontal analysis (the year-on-year change), the margin ladder, and the revenue-to-net-profit bridge. The statement does not report an operating-profit subtotal, so the margin ladder shows gross and net only. The segmental split is disclosed on a six-month basis, and the manufacturing arm sells mostly to the group's own stores, so its profit contribution is shown rather than a margin. The quarterly trend uses profit after tax. EBITDA is the company's disclosed figure. Figures in RM'000.

Delay Analysis Insights

Weak Upward Trend Over Time
Analysis shows delays have been increasing slightly over time with a correlation of +0.30. However, this weak trend is overshadowed by much stronger seasonal factors like holidays (+0.73) and events (+0.59), making predictable patterns more actionable than time-based trends.
Critical Days: Fridays and Sundays
Delays vary significantly by day of week. Midweek days (Tuesday-Thursday) average 12-13 minutes, while Fridays and Sundays peak at 14.3-14.4 minutes. This reflects end-of-week commuting and weekend travel demand, requiring targeted resource allocation.
Holidays: The Strongest Delay Driver
Holidays emerge as the single largest delay source, adding almost 30 minutes compared to non-holidays. With a correlation of +0.73 and p-value of 0.007, this effect is both strong and statistically reliable, making holidays the top planning priority.
Event Impact: Moderate but Disruptive
Large events show a moderate correlation of +0.59 with delays. While less predictable than holidays, high-attendance events can create sudden spikes that disrupt normal operations, requiring advance monitoring and contingency planning.
Linear Regression: Best Forecasting Model
Linear regression outperforms other models, explaining about 10% of delay variation while producing practical, minute-based forecasts. It translates into actionable insights like "expect an extra 28 minutes on holidays," making it ideal for operational planning.
Model Accuracy and Limitations
The model explains 9.5% of variation with average error of 2.4 minutes. While not highly accurate for extreme events, it reliably captures baseline delays of 12-14 minutes and is most effective when seasonality and holidays are factored in.

Predictive Analytics

Prediction 1: Future Delay Forecast
Model forecasts stable delays of 12-14 minutes over the next two weeks under normal conditions, providing operations managers a reliable baseline for schedule planning with adjustments needed only for holidays or special events.
Prediction 2: What-If Scenario Analysis
When a 10,000-attendee event coincides with a holiday, delays increase from 13.4 to 14.7 minutes (10% rise). While small per trip, the cumulative effect across hundreds of services requires advance preparation and resource allocation.
Prediction 3: Model Fit Evaluation
For January's first three weeks, the model predicted 12-15 minutes with 0.70 correlation to actual delays (0-30 minute range). The model captures normal conditions but misses extreme spikes, requiring additional buffers for unusual disruption days.
Prediction 4: Intervention Impact Analysis
During fuel price increase week, delays decreased slightly (-0.9 minutes) with weak statistical significance (p=0.056). This suggests fuel policy changes have minimal impact compared to holidays and events, which should remain the primary planning focus.

Operational Recommendations

Strategic Resource Planning
Prioritize resource allocation for Fridays, Sundays, and especially holidays. Deploy extra staff, vehicles, and buffer time on these critical days. Holidays require 30-minute buffers compared to 12-14 minutes on normal days, making them the single most important planning factor.
Dynamic Timetable Adjustment
Use the regression model to forecast delays for upcoming periods and adjust schedules accordingly. Build baseline buffers of 12-14 minutes into normal schedules, expanding to 30 minutes for holidays. Test what-if scenarios when events coincide with peak days.
Event Monitoring & Contingency Plans
Monitor event calendars closely for high-attendance occasions. While less predictable than holidays, events with 10,000+ attendees can increase delays by 10%. Develop contingency plans and deploy additional resources when events overlap with Fridays, Sundays, or holidays.
Passenger Communication Strategy
Translate forecasts into simple risk categories (low/moderate/high) for passenger understanding. Share advance warnings at least one week before holidays or major events. Provide real-time updates on the day of travel to help passengers make informed decisions and build trust in the service.

What the engines reveal

DRB-HICOM’s surge was cost efficiency (+93% of last year’s profit) and other income (+34%), not revenue (+12%); a sharp drop in associate income (−31%) clawed a third back.

Press Metal ran on the aluminium spread (+52%); a collapse in associate income (−20%) took a chunk back.

Tenaga was nearly flat: modest volume (+10%) offset by a finance-income drop (−9%).

None of the three grew on volume alone, and two carry a large associate-related drag. The engines differ even where the headline (profit up) looks the same.

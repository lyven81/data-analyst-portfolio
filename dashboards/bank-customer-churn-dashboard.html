Key Customer Insights

Q1: Key Churn Factors Identified
Age, geography (Germany), balance, activity status, and gender are the most important factors linked to customer leaving, while other variables add little predictive value.
Q2: Focus on Seven Significant Predictors
Relationship managers should prioritize age, activity status, Germany location, gender, balance, credit score, and number of products as reliable churn indicators.
Q3: Reliable Predictive Model Built
The logistic regression model provides moderate explanatory power and is stable enough to guide retention strategies for prioritizing high-risk customers.
Q4: Individual Probability Scoring
Every customer receives a probability score between 0-1, with customers above 30% (like customer 1003 at 34%) requiring immediate attention from managers.
Q5: Customer-Specific Risk Assessment
Individual risk scores enable targeted outreach, with customer 2840 showing highest risk (19.3%) and inactive customers requiring priority monitoring.
Q6: Activity Status as Strongest Lever
Becoming inactive sharply raises churn risk while product additions lower it. German customers need special attention as they're twice as likely to leave.
Q7: Highly Accurate Prediction Performance
Model correctly predicts about 86% of customers, getting 8 to 9 out of every 10 predictions right. This high accuracy makes it reliable for guiding retention decisions and resource allocation.

Strategic Retention Recommendations

Implement Tiered Response Strategy
Focus immediate attention on high-risk customers (>30% probability), personalized outreach for moderate-risk (15-30%), and regular communication for low-risk segments.
Prioritize Activity Engagement
Develop targeted campaigns to re-engage inactive customers through personalized offers, digital banking incentives, and relationship manager outreach programs.
Geographic Customization
Create Germany-specific retention strategies addressing unique market conditions, competitive pressures, and customer expectations in the German banking sector.
Product Portfolio Expansion
Encourage additional product adoption through needs-based consultations, as customers with multiple products show significantly lower churn propensity.

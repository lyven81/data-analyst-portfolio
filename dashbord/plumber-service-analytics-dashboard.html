Plumber Service Analytics Dashboard

Transforming Raw CRM Data into Actionable Productivity Insights

Overview

A home services plumbing company with 10 technicians was struggling to track performance and identify quality issues. Despite having a CRM system, they had no visibility into key operational metrics like technician utilization, callback rates, or weekly trends. Management relied on gut feel rather than data, missing opportunities to improve efficiency and reduce costly rework.

The Business Challenge: How can a small plumbing business gain operational visibility without hiring a dedicated analyst or spending hours on manual reporting?

Project Goal: Build a repeatable productivity analytics system that tracks company-wide and individual technician performance, identifies quality issues (callbacks), and supports weekly decision-making‚Äîall with minimal manual intervention.

Tools Used: Python (Pandas, NumPy), Google Looker Studio, CSV data processing, Business logic design

1. Who Has the Business Problem?

The Client: A plumbing services company managing 10 field technicians handling 200-300 jobs per month across service, repair, and installation work.

Pain Points Identified:

  • No visibility into productivity: Management couldn't see which technicians were efficient vs. underperforming
  • Hidden quality issues: Callbacks (repeat visits for the same problem) weren't tracked, costing thousands in unpaid rework
  • Time-consuming manual reporting: Pulling basic metrics required hours of Excel work each week
  • Gut-feel decisions: Technician performance reviews and job assignments based on perception, not data
  • No trend analysis: Unable to see if business performance was improving or declining week-over-week

Business Impact: Low utilization (technicians spending too much time on non-billable activities), undetected quality issues leading to customer dissatisfaction, and lack of data-driven coaching opportunities meant leaving $30,000+ in potential annual revenue on the table.

Key Business Need:

Transform raw CRM exports into a weekly dashboard that shows: (1) company-wide utilization and revenue trends, (2) individual technician performance and quality metrics, and (3) early warning indicators for quality issues‚Äîwith zero manual data entry.

5. Solution: Callback Detection & Quality Improvement

Callbacks‚Äîwhen a technician must return to fix the same problem‚Äîare invisible profit killers. The CRM doesn't flag them, but our Callback Drill-Down page automatically detects and analyzes them.

Automated Callback Detection Logic: A job is flagged as a callback if: (1) same customer as previous job, (2) return visit within 30 days, (3) job notes mention "same issue" or "customer reported problem again," and (4) NOT a scheduled follow-up or routine maintenance.

June 2024 Results: Detected 15 callbacks (6.7% of jobs)‚Äîabove the 5% target. Without this dashboard, these would have gone unnoticed, costing ~$250 per callback in unpaid technician time = $3,750/month = $45,000/year in hidden waste.

Detailed Callback Table: Every callback job listed with: Job ID, date, technician, customer, job type, and reason flagged. Managers can review each case individually and discuss with techs.

Root Cause Analysis Views:

  • Callbacks by Technician: Chart showing Tech #7 has 5 callbacks (12% rate) vs. Tech #5 with 1 callback (3% rate)‚Äîpinpointing where training is needed
  • Callbacks by Job Type: Are most callbacks on repairs? Installs? Reveals if there's a skills gap in a specific service area

Action Workflow: Each Monday, manager reviews new callbacks, discusses with involved technicians ("What happened on Job #0234? How can we prevent this?"), and tracks if interventions reduce future callbacks.

Quality ROI:

Reducing callback rate from 6.7% to 4.5% eliminates ~5 callbacks per month. At $250 cost per callback (tech time, fuel, parts), that's $1,250/month = $15,000/year savings. Plus improved customer satisfaction leads to more referrals and better reviews.

6. How It Was Built: Data Processing & Business Logic

The Challenge: CRM systems export messy, siloed data across multiple files. Building a dashboard requires cleaning, merging, and calculating metrics that don't exist in raw data.

Data Sources (4 CSV Files):

  • jobs.csv: 300 work orders with dates, technicians, customers, job categories, and status
  • invoices.csv: 225 billing records with billable hours, hourly rates, and invoice amounts
  • time_logs.csv: 300 detailed time breakdowns (work hours, travel hours, idle hours)
  • technicians.csv: 10 technician profiles with names and service types

Data Processing Pipeline (Python Script):

  1. Load & Clean: Import all 4 CSVs, fix date formatting issues, handle missing values
  2. Filter: Remove 75 cancelled jobs (only analyze completed work)
  3. Merge: Join jobs + invoices + time logs + technicians using job_id and technician_id keys
  4. Calculate Metrics:
    • Utilization = billable_hours √∑ paid_hours √ó 100
    • Revenue per billable hour = invoice_amount √∑ billable_hours
    • Time allocation percentages (work%, travel%, idle%)
  5. Detect Callbacks: Apply business rules (customer revisits + time window + job notes analysis)
  6. Aggregate: Create 3 datasets: job-level (225 rows), technician-level (10 rows), weekly-level (30 rows)
  7. Export: Save clean CSVs ready for dashboard connection

Processing Time: Under 5 seconds to transform 300 jobs. Fully repeatable‚Äîsame script runs weekly on new data exports.

Technical Excellence:

The magic isn't in fancy algorithms‚Äîit's in thoughtful business logic design. Every calculation is documented, every assumption justified, every edge case handled. This makes the system auditable, trustworthy, and maintainable.

7. How It Was Built: Dashboard Design Principles

Design Philosophy: Dashboards fail when they're overcomplicated. Our approach: Show the fewest possible metrics that drive the most action.

4-Page Structure (Looker Studio):

  1. Company Overview: 4 top-line scorecards + 3 charts = everything management needs in 10 seconds
  2. Technician Comparison: Performance table + 2 comparison charts = basis for coaching conversations
  3. Weekly Trends: 4 time-series charts = answer "are we improving?"
  4. Callback Drill-Down: Detail table + 2 analysis charts = quality improvement deep-dive

User Experience Considerations:

  • 3-Click Rule: Any question should be answerable in 3 clicks or less
  • Color Psychology: Green = good, Amber = watch, Red = action needed (traffic light system everyone understands)
  • Context Always Visible: Global filters (date range, technician, job type) appear on every page
  • Mobile-Friendly: Scorecards stack vertically, charts resize, tables scroll horizontally on phones

Interactivity Features:

  • Click a technician's name ‚Üí filter entire dashboard to that person
  • Click a week on trends page ‚Üí drill down to that week's jobs
  • Hover over any chart ‚Üí see detailed tooltip with exact values

Built for Non-Technical Users: The owner isn't a data analyst. The dashboard uses plain language labels ("Good utilization" not "71.6th percentile"), shows "what good looks like" with reference lines, and includes hover hints explaining each metric.

Design Impact:

A well-designed dashboard gets used. A confusing one gets ignored. By prioritizing clarity over complexity, we ensured this dashboard would become part of the weekly management routine‚Äînot a one-time curiosity that gets abandoned.
